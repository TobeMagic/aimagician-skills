# Venue Selection Workflow

Use this workflow for journal/conference ranking, SCI/EI/Chinese core/CCF/OA decisions, graduation-safe fallback planning, and target shortlist creation.

## Gather Hard Rules First

Do not select venues before reading the hard constraints:

- school graduation requirement
- department recognition list
- advisor/group requirement
- scholarship or award rules
- whitelist/blacklist/warning list
- whether conferences count
- whether OA counts
- whether online-first counts
- deadline for acceptance vs publication vs indexing

School recognition overrides generic labels.

## Candidate Discovery

Find candidates from:

- venues of recent papers in the field
- references of target papers
- CNKI for Chinese journals
- LetPub/JCR/CAS/JCR official sources for SCI where accessible
- CCF official list for computer science
- publisher/journal official sites
- advisor or senior student history

Create a shortlist table:

```csv
venue,kind,field_fit,recognized_by_school,level,indexing,oa,apc,review_speed,format_burden,recent_related_papers,risk,fallback_rank,notes
```

## Ranking Concepts

Common venue labels:

- Chinese ordinary journal
- Chinese core: Peking University Core, CSSCI, CSTPCD, CSCD, and other recognized core lists
- SCI / SCIE / SSCI / A&HCI
- EI journal
- EI conference / CPCI / ISTP
- CCF A/B/C conference or journal
- school internal A/B/C/D, top, authoritative
- OA journal

For computer science:

- CCF and SCI answer different questions
- CCF is field-community ranking
- SCI partition is journal-impact based
- some schools recognize one more than the other

## Decision Routes

Graduation only:

- target the minimum recognized level
- prioritize recognition certainty and review speed
- avoid overinvesting in top venues if the paper does not affect future goals

Job-oriented:

- choose the safest recognized route
- avoid long review cycles if paper prestige has no job impact
- keep experiments and writing scoped to the venue

PhD / academic track:

- target SCI Q1/top, CCF A/B, or field-equivalent venues
- use stronger baselines and deeper experiments
- invest in English polish and story quality
- keep a fallback ladder in case timelines slip

Advisor requires high level:

- if time allows, try the advisor-preferred level once
- define a deadline for decision/rejection
- prepare a lower-level fallback venue
- communicate with evidence: review time, graduation deadline, paper fit

Urgent timeline:

- prioritize fast, recognized, scope-fit venues
- verify whether acceptance, publication, or indexing is required
- avoid venues with uncertain indexing if indexing is required
- reduce claims and target level rather than cutting corners

## OA Venue Checks

OA can be legitimate, but check:

- school recognition
- official indexing
- warning/predatory list status
- APC and who pays
- publisher reputation
- review process credibility
- recent paper quality
- special issue risk
- timeline realism

Do not equate "fast" with "safe."

## EI Conference Checks

Check:

- whether the school recognizes EI conferences
- whether acceptance or EI indexing is required
- conference history
- proceedings publisher
- previous indexing record
- topic fit
- fee
- duplicate/predatory warning signs

Acceptance is not the same as indexing.

## Rejection Fallback

Before first submission, define:

- first target
- second target
- emergency target
- maximum wait time
- what will be revised after rejection

Fallback rules:

- if paper has long-term value: drop one level only if needed
- if graduation-only: revise and resubmit to same/lower realistic level
- if PhD: stay ambitious if time allows
- if reviews reveal a serious flaw: fix evidence before resubmission
