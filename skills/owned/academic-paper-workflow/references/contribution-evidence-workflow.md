# Contribution Evidence Workflow

Use this for contribution framing, innovation statements, title/abstract/introduction, claim boundaries, and evidence alignment.

## Contribution Is Evidence Order

A persuasive paper lets reviewers see:

```text
real problem -> current limitation -> proposed method -> why it should work -> evidence -> limitation
```

Never create a contribution claim that outruns the evidence.

## CARS-Style Introduction

Use a CARS-like chain:

1. Establish territory: why the area/task matters.
2. Establish niche: what gap, limitation, contradiction, or unmet condition remains.
3. Occupy niche: what this paper contributes and how it is evaluated.

For each move, attach citations or evidence. If the gap only exists because the literature map is incomplete, go back to literature mapping.

## Take-Home Message

Reduce the paper to one sentence:

```text
For [task/context], existing [method family] struggles with [specific limitation], so we propose [core method], which improves [evidence target] under [scope].
```

If this sentence needs three unrelated ideas, the paper is probably overstacked.

## Contribution Types

Choose the contribution type before writing:

- method: a component, architecture, algorithm, or protocol
- empirical: a finding, failure analysis, comparison, or benchmark insight
- resource: dataset, annotation, benchmark, toolkit
- application: adapting known methods to a concrete scenario
- review/survey: taxonomy, synthesis, or research agenda
- thesis integration: organizing multiple paper outputs into one coherent research line

Contribution wording should include both novelty and boundary:

- strong: "We propose X to address Y and evaluate it on Z."
- cautious: "We show that X can improve Y under Z."
- unsafe: "We are the first/SOTA/universal" without exhaustive evidence.

## Claim-Evidence-Limitation Map

Build this before drafting:

| Claim | Evidence | Figure/Table | Section | Limitation |
|---|---|---|---|---|

Rules:

- every claim needs evidence
- every major evidence item needs a figure, table, or exact textual result
- limitations should qualify claims near where they appear
- do not call a comparison comprehensive unless inclusion criteria justify it

## Innovation Packaging

Innovation is strongest when it has:

- novelty relative to recent literature
- usefulness for a known task or audience
- measurable improvement or insight
- clear mechanism or explanation
- honest scope boundary

If novelty is modest, shift emphasis to evidence quality, application value, benchmark/resource contribution, or practical constraints.

## Writing Outputs

For contribution work, output:

- take-home message
- CARS introduction outline
- contribution list
- claim-evidence-limitation map
- title options
- abstract skeleton

For target-venue adaptation, compare 5-10 accepted papers and learn structure, claim scale, and evidence style, not wording.
