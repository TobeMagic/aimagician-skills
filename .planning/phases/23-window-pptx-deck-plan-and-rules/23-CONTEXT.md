# Phase 23 Context: DeckPlan and Semantic Rules

Phase 22 established safe, transactional PowerPoint execution. Phase 23 moves the content-planning decisions that ordinary models handle inconsistently into a pure-Python semantic compiler.

The model supplies business intent, facts, content blocks, and optional preferences. It may not supply coordinates, font sizes, font names, raw colors, COM calls, arbitrary code, or unregistered layout IDs. The compiler selects a narrative archetype, derives slide roles, maps semantics to ranked page forms, enforces capacity, and records every decision.

Required scenarios are business report, project proposal, product launch, market analysis, sales proposal, investor pitch, annual review, strategic plan, data analysis, research report, training, brand introduction, project kickoff, operations review, and e-commerce/marketing plan.

Phase 23 does not implement pixel geometry, themes, PowerPoint shapes, advanced objects, or visual QA. It produces a stable compiled semantic representation consumed by Phase 24 and later phases.
